# Target-Sales-Dashboard-Online-vs-In-Store-Performance-Analysis-
Interactive Excel dashboard analyzing Target's Online (Pickup/Delivery) vs In-Store sales performance across Australian states. Tracks revenue, category performance, and regional trends using dynamic KPIs, slicers, and sparklines.

## Objective
•	Consolidate raw order-level data into a clean, analyzable dataset
•	Quantify the revenue split between digital and physical retail channels
•	Identify which states, suburbs, categories, and staff are driving (or lagging in) performance
•	Package the analysis into a self-service dashboard for non-technical stakeholders

## Key components of the analysis:
•	KPI summary cards for Total Sales, Average Sales, Top Suburb, Top Buyer, and Top Store Manager
•	Time-series analysis of Total Sales and Average Sales, split by Online (red) vs In-Store (black)
•	Category-wise sales breakdown across 10 product categories
•	State-level performance table with sales trend sparklines
•	Store manager and top buyer performance rankings
•	Fully interactive slicers for Channel Type, Financial Year, State, and Category

## Business Questions Answered
•	What is the total sales revenue?
•	How are sales trending over time?
•	What is the average sales value?
•	Which suburb generates the highest sales?
•	Which sales manager performs the best?
•	Who is the top buyer?
•	Which product categories contribute the most revenue?
•	How do Store sales compare with E-Retail sales?
•	Which Australian states generate the highest sales?

## Key Insights:
💰 Total Sales: $1.85M+ ($1,855,896)
📊 Average Sales Value: $412
📈 Sales Trend: Revenue held relatively stable across FY2024–2025 with month-to-month fluctuation rather than strong seasonality; In-Store consistently formed the larger base layer over Online in every month
🏆 Top Performing Suburb: Campbelltown
👨‍💼 Top Sales Manager: Fatima Saleh
👥 Top Buyer: Ibrahim Khalil
🛍️ Store vs Online: Approximately 70% of sales come from physical (Bricks & Mortar) stores, while 30% come from Online Pickup/Delivery
🌏 State-wise Analysis: New South Wales is the strongest performing state ($545,938), followed by Queensland ($393,106); Northern Territory and ACT trail well behind, reflecting a clear population-density effect
📦 Category Analysis: Furniture and Grocery are the top revenue-generating categories, while Movie, Music & Books contributes the least — a candidate for range review or bundling
👥 Buyer & Manager Performance: Manager rankings (led by Fatima Saleh, followed by Clementine Walsh and Lucas Le) and top-10 buyer tracking support performance recognition and strategic planning at the store level

## Business Recommendations
•	Investigate stores that under-index on Online sales relative to the 70/30 average — these may be under-promoting Pickup/Delivery or facing fulfillment friction.
•	Consider bundling low-performing categories (e.g., Movies/Music/Books) with high-traffic categories (Grocery, Furniture) to lift attachment rate.
•	Use top-performing managers as a benchmark for a best-practice playbook across underperforming stores.
